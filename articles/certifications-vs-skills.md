# Stop Collecting Certificates! Focus on Real Skills Instead.

Recently, I talked to some people who were looking for jobs. They showed me **30 to 40 course certificates** and thought they would get a job easily. 

When I told them, **"These certificates have no value,"** they got angry at me. But then I explained the truth.

### 1. "Watching" is not "Doing"
A **Course Completion Certificate** only means you watched some videos. It does not prove you have the skills. The certificates that actually matter are **Exam-Based Certifications** like **OSCP, eJPT, PJPT, or BTL1**. These are hard to get because you have to pass a real practical test.

### 2. Don’t be a "Collector"
Many students think: *"More certificates = Better job."* This is a big myth. Recruiters don't want to see 40 random PDFs. They want to see what you can actually do. 
* **5 to 7 good certificates** are more than enough.
* Spend your time on **Labs** and **Practice**, not just clicking "Next" on a video.

### 3. Choose One Path
If you try to learn everything at once (Red Team, Blue Team, GRC), you will learn nothing well.
* If you want to be a **Hacker (Red Team)**, focus on that.
* If you want to be a **Defender (Blue Team)**, focus on that.

**Stop counting certificates. Start building skills.**
